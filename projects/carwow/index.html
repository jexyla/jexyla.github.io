carwow is the most convenient, stress-free way to find and buy a new car, at a price you can be confident in.

Overview

carwow aims to solve the major pain points that buyers and dealers experience in the buying process, putting them in a more powerful position than ever before. It provides consumers with the ability to make an informed decision when buying a car at each stage of the buying journey.

And it's great news for dealers too. Through carwow, dealer partners sell more cars than they could in the showroom alone, while reducing cost inefficiencies and increasing stock turnover. This means they can pass on great prices to carwow buyers.

My role

As a part of the Product Supply team at carwow, I was the main designer for the dealership facing website where carwow’s dealers control the cars they have in stock and keep in touch with customers.

We’ve initially spent a lot of time focusing on the customer facing website, so I strive to make the dealer-facing site better and better. I believe that if we can continue to work more on it and make it as simple and easy to use as possible, it can be a real asset to their businesses.

Also, by working in the Product Supply team, I had the opportunity to experience cross-functional collaboration, working especially closely with data and developers.

Dealer Engagement

One of the main objectives for the Product Supply team was to “increase dealer engagement on the site” as more engaged dealers means better performing dealers.

User research

To support this we thought about hosting an in-house Dealer Focus Group session, focused mainly on enquiry handling, to discover the actual main pain points with the current layout and propose new ideas.This way we can have a better sense of what we can improve and how we can do it and validate some of our assumptions. But to have a better sense of how we can improve, it would be helpful to know better our users and the way they work. For the focus group, it would be good to have a mixed group of dealers so that we can easily target different problems for different cases. The ideal mix would be the following: - Newly on-boarded dealers and Long-time user dealers - Low Volume: High performing dealer and Low performing dealer - High Volume: High performing dealer and Low performing dealer - [Optional] Mid Volume: High performing dealer and Low performing dealer
